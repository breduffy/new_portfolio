UX/Art/Creative Direction for Personal Finance Game

This educational program aims to teach college level students about personal finance—an often overlooked but crucial life skill. The scenario based game presents the user with different financial choices and shows them a potential outcome of those choices. is actually like three games in one aimed at different demographics. The three games are: a road-trip, a semester abroad in Italy, and a year off from school. I developed the concept, created the UX design and templates, worked with subject matter experts to develop the content, and directed a team of illustrators and developers in India to produce over 100 screens.

Nope Don't Care

Nope Don't Care was created as a humorous group side project. The program is an anti-social network—because one can't like everything. Users can posts photos that they find particularly vexacious, dislike other user's 'nopes', follow other users, tag inappropriate content such as baby photos, and listen to white noise when the internet becomes too overwhelming. I completed all of the front-end template development in Jade, the interaction design, UI/UX, and graphic design. I also contributed to the back-end code.


Note: This project is under construction by team Lovelace.

Art City

Art city is an art finder that allows users to upload interesting art in their city or search for art within a particular city. Art City is a work in progress. Users can filter art by a category. Version two will include Google Maps API for walking tours.

Note: Please allow enough time for heroku to start up before searching for art. For now it takes time.

Monster Maps

Monster Maps is a fun experiment in several techniques I wanted to learn, and thus makes very little sense. Current functionality allows the user to draw a background, drag and drop monster parts, and save their monster in a new window. The user can also place a marker on the map. V2 will allow the monster to be saved at the location.

Note: There are small issues with the drag and drop section and the map area, which I am in the process of fixing.
